# News Catalyst Filter

Use this reference when judging whether the latest related news changes an A-share bottom-stabilization, first-tier-theme, or trend-entry conclusion.

## Core Principle

News does not replace price, volume, sector breadth, and index risk. News only changes confidence after the market confirms or rejects it.

The practical sequence is:

1. Identify the news.
2. Classify its strength.
3. Map it to the target stock and sector.
4. Check whether price-volume confirms.
5. Upgrade, downgrade, or ignore the trend judgment.

## News Strength Classification

### Hard Catalyst

Higher weight, especially if fresh and directly linked to the target.

Examples:

- Official policy or regulator document.
- Listed-company announcement.
- Confirmed major contract, order, tender win, or delivery.
- Earnings upgrade, margin expansion, capacity expansion, or capex cycle.
- Industry standard, license, approval, procurement, or subsidy with named beneficiaries.

How to use:

- Upgrade trend quality only if the stock and sector show volume-backed acceptance.
- If the stock opens high and fades with heavy volume, treat as possible "sell the news".

### Soft Catalyst

Medium or low weight.

Examples:

- Media interpretation.
- Brokerage theme framing.
- Conference or product-launch expectations.
- Overseas mapping from US AI / semiconductor / cloud capex.
- Industry rumors or unconfirmed supply-chain talk.

How to use:

- Useful for watchlist ranking.
- Not enough for trend-entry by itself.
- Requires at least 2 sessions of sector acceptance.

### Risk News

High weight when it aligns with weak price action.

Examples:

- Regulatory inquiry or punishment.
- Major shareholder reduction.
- Earnings miss or order cancellation.
- Sanctions, export controls, tariffs, or supply-chain restriction.
- Litigation, safety incident, customer loss, or financing pressure.

How to use:

- Downgrade faster when VIX rises, indexes weaken, or the stock loses support with volume.
- A risk headline ignored by price for 1-2 sessions is less important, but still monitor.

### Noise

Low or no weight.

Examples:

- Repeated old news.
- Vague market talk.
- Non-specific "benefiting from AI" language with no direct stock linkage.
- News that affects the broad theme but not the target's actual business segment.

How to use:

- Do not upgrade a stock to first-tier or trend-entry based on noise.

## Market Confirmation Rules

A bullish news catalyst is valid only when most of the following appear:

- Target closes near high or holds key trigger after the news.
- Turnover rises but does not become exhaustion volume.
- Sector leaders and peer stocks follow.
- Intraday pullbacks are bought.
- Index risk does not worsen at the same time.

A bullish news catalyst is suspect when:

- The stock gaps up and closes near the low.
- Sector peers do not follow.
- Volume expands but price cannot advance.
- The news is widely discussed after a large prior rally.
- US technology / SOX / VIX filter is adverse for high-position technology.

## Zhongtian Technology 600522 Mapping

For Zhongtian Technology, separate direct and indirect catalysts.

Direct catalysts:

- Optical fiber and cable demand.
- Optical communication infrastructure.
- Telecom infrastructure and 6G construction.
- Submarine cable / offshore wind / marine energy engineering.
- Power grid, new-energy infrastructure, and energy storage.
- Confirmed company order, tender, capacity, earnings, or announcement.

Indirect catalysts:

- CPO and optical-module strength.
- US AI capex and cloud infrastructure.
- Semiconductor or PCB theme strength.

Implication:

- Direct catalysts can support Zhongtian's own trend judgment.
- Indirect CPO / optical-module news mainly supports sector sentiment. It does not make Zhongtian a pure CPO core leader.

## First-Tier Status Update Rule

A stock stays in first-tier observation only if news and market behavior agree:

- Its theme remains in the recent capital main line.
- The stock remains among high-turnover, high-recognition peers.
- Direct or indirect catalysts continue appearing.
- Peer leaders stop falling or repair together.
- Key support or trigger levels hold.

Downgrade the stock if:

- News turns negative or loses freshness.
- Sector news is strong but the stock stops responding.
- Peer leaders continue weakening.
- Capital rotates to another theme for multiple sessions.
- The stock breaks support with volume.
